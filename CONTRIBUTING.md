# Contributing to AVAR

**License:** CC BY 4.0

Thanks for your interest in the AVAR standard.

## Types of Contribution

- **RFC proposals** — normative changes to the spec (see GOVERNANCE.md)
- **Editorial PRs** — typos, clarifications, examples
- **Test vectors** — additions to `Aarmatix/avar-conformance`
- **Reference verifier fixes** — bug fixes in `Aarmatix/avar`

## Before You Open a PR

1. For normative changes: open an issue labeled `rfc-proposal` first.
2. For editorial changes: PRs welcome directly.
3. Sign commits with `git commit -s` (DCO 1.1).

## DCO

By signing off your commits, you certify the [Developer Certificate of
Origin 1.1](https://developercertificate.org/). No CLA is required.

## Review

Editors review editorial PRs within 14 days. RFC PRs follow the timeline in
GOVERNANCE.md.

## Style

- Spec text is Markdown, US English, MUST/SHOULD/MAY per RFC 2119.
- Line length ≤ 80 characters where practical.
- Numbered sections; no emoji in normative text.

## What NOT to Contribute Here

- Aarmos-specific policies, adapters, or runtime code (that lives in the
  private Aarmos monorepo)
- Performance benchmarks tied to a specific implementation (belongs in that
  implementation's repo)
- Marketing content
